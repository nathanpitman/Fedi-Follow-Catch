Fedi Follow Fetch

Find the gaps between who you follow on Bluesky and who you follow on Mastodon.

How this works, and what it does with your credentials

  • No backend. This is a static page. There is no server of ours involved anywhere.
  • Your password and token are never stored. They live only in this browser tab's memory — refreshing or closing the page erases them, and they're never written to a database, cookie, or localStorage. If you tick "remember my handle & instance" below, only those two non-secret fields are saved to this browser's localStorage for 30 days, purely so you don't have to retype them next time.
  • Direct calls only. Your browser talks straight to bsky.social and to your own Mastodon instance's API. We never see your credentials, and they are never logged anywhere.
  • Read-only. This tool never follows, unfollows, or posts anything. It only reads your two follow lists and shows you the difference — you decide what to do about it.

Don't take our word for it — read the source on GitHub.

Connect Bluesky

Never sent to any server but Bluesky's. Use an app password, not your main password — you can revoke it any time.

Connect Mastodon

Never sent to any server but your instance's. On your instance, go to Preferences → Development → New Application, give it any name, tick only read:accounts and read:follows, submit, then copy "Your access token". See Mastodon's guide if you get stuck.
